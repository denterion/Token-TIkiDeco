# Reviewer Conflict Policy

## Required Disclosure

A prospective reviewer must disclose any current or recent financial, employment, advisory, family, governance or commercial relationship with the maintainer, treasury controllers, proposed signers or any future operator under consideration.

## Disqualifying Or Escalated Conflicts

- ownership or control of a reviewed privileged address;
- participation in treasury or token-allocation decisions;
- compensation based on listing, market activity, token distribution, finding count or review outcome;
- undisclosed subcontracting;
- pressure to omit, downgrade or delay a material finding;
- request for private keys, seed phrases or transaction authority.

A disclosed minor conflict requires a written independence decision before engagement. A material unresolved conflict disqualifies the reviewer.

## Reviewer Independence Record

Record reviewer identity or firm, disclosed conflicts, compensation model, subcontractors, reviewer access, scope, decision owner and date. Do not publish sensitive personal or security information.

This policy does not establish that a reviewer has been selected or that an independent review has started.
