# TikiDeco Public Communication Policy

This policy defines safe public language for the TikiDeco / TIDE prototype.

It is not legal advice. Any public sale, fundraising campaign, mainnet launch, or regulated offering decision must be reviewed by qualified counsel.

## Core Positioning

TIDE may be described as:

- a public Sepolia testnet prototype
- a hospitality-linked token concept
- a community and transparency layer
- a future utility experiment subject to legal review
- a fixed-supply ERC-20 compatible token prototype
- a Safe-administered testnet project

TIDE should not be described as:

- an investment opportunity
- hotel equity
- real estate ownership
- revenue share
- dividend right
- debt claim
- guaranteed token appreciation
- guaranteed hotel access
- guaranteed mainnet launch
- regulator-approved product

## Approved Short Description

```text
TikiDeco / TIDE is a Sepolia testnet prototype for a future hospitality-linked token concept connected to a Miami Beach hotel vision. It is designed around fixed supply, vesting, Safe governance, and on-chain transparency reports. It is not an investment offer or promise of profit.
```

## Social Post Template

```text
TikiDeco / TIDE update:

- Sepolia token and vesting vault are deployed and verified
- Owner control is now held by a Safe multisig
- A transparency report hash has been published on-chain
- OpenZeppelin V2 contract track is merged for review

Testnet prototype only. No profit, equity, revenue share, or investment return is promised.
```

## Partner Conversation Boundary

When discussing partners, use:

- pilot collaboration
- hospitality utility exploration
- community engagement
- perks design
- technical prototype
- legal review pending

Avoid:

- investor allocation
- guaranteed upside
- token-backed hotel ownership
- buy before launch
- share of hotel revenue
- expected token price

## Website And README Boundary

Every public surface should make clear:

- Sepolia tokens have no market value
- TIDE is not live on mainnet
- public sale is not approved
- utility is subject to legal and operational review
- hotel-related perks are future concepts, not guaranteed rights

## Review Process

Before publishing material updates:

1. Check that the wording avoids investment promises.
2. Check that any contract addresses and transaction hashes are correct.
3. Check that any report hash matches the final report file.
4. Check that legal boundary language is present.
5. If the update mentions fundraising, sale, contributors, hotel economics, ownership, revenue, or profits, stop and get legal review first.

## Incident Language

If a contract, wallet, website, or reporting issue is found:

- state what is known
- state what is not yet known
- avoid speculation
- avoid blaming users or partners
- publish transaction hashes and timelines where possible
- publish a follow-up report after remediation
