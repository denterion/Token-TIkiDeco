# TIDE Loyalty Pilot

Status: planned pilot specification. Not live. Sepolia-only. No sale, no stated monetary value, no mainnet deployment, and independent audit not started.

## Pilot Objective

Test whether TIDE can support transparent, limited eligibility workflows for future hospitality-linked community programs without creating financial rights, sale language, or active hotel benefit claims.

## Target Users

- Public community members who want to understand the TikiDeco prototype.
- Testnet participants who can verify wallet control through message signing.
- Internal operators reviewing whether token-based eligibility can be handled clearly and safely.
- Future counsel/security reviewers evaluating pilot rules before any live operational use.

## Pilot Scope

- Ethereum Sepolia only.
- Read-only public verification pages.
- Wallet message signing for optional eligibility verification.
- Balance snapshot against a published campaign block.
- Manual review before any pilot perk is confirmed.
- Limited non-cash perks only where legally and operationally allowed.
- Public pilot reports that avoid exposing private participant data.

## Out Of Scope

- No token sale, presale, paid access, redemption, or exchange activity.
- Mainnet deployment or mainnet token claims.
- No financial value, price, APY, yield, staking, or expected return.
- No equity, debt, revenue, property, hotel ownership, dividend, or profit rights.
- Guaranteed benefit, booking, room access, discount, membership, or hospitality service.
- Automated acceptance without manual review.
- Collection of unnecessary private data.

## Sepolia-Only Status

All pilot references are testnet-only unless a later legal-approved document and governance record says otherwise. V1 remains the historical canonical Sepolia deployment. V2 remains candidate code only.

## No-Sale / No-Value Disclaimer

TIDE is not offered for sale, has no stated monetary value, is not deployed on mainnet, and has not completed an independent audit. Nothing in this pilot is financial, investment, legal, or tax advice.

## Eligible Activities

Eligibility activities may include:

- signing a one-time verification message;
- holding at least the published minimum Sepolia balance at the campaign snapshot block;
- submitting a pilot RSVP request during an allowed window;
- passing duplicate-wallet, spam, sanctions, abuse, and manual review checks where applicable;
- accepting pilot terms and privacy disclosures before any operational test.

## Possible Non-Cash Perks

Pilot-safe categories may include:

- an early RSVP window for a selected community preview;
- priority access review for selected community preview events;
- non-cash welcome eligibility where legally allowed.

All categories are limited, conditional, non-transferable unless rules say otherwise, and have no resale value or cash redemption.

## Inventory Limits

Each campaign must publish:

- maximum available slots or units;
- eligibility threshold;
- snapshot block;
- review window;
- cancellation rules;
- dispute deadline;
- report publication target.

If requests exceed inventory, staff may use a documented allocation method such as first eligible request, lottery among eligible requests, or manual priority review. The method must be published before the campaign opens.

## Blackout Dates

Campaign rules may exclude dates or times because of venue limits, staff availability, legal restrictions, holidays, safety constraints, or operational conflicts.

Blackout dates should be published before the campaign opens when known. If a blackout date is added later, the pilot report should explain the reason without exposing private participant data.

## Manual Review

Manual review is required before any perk is confirmed. Review may check:

- wallet signature validity;
- snapshot balance;
- duplicate wallet or spam patterns;
- campaign inventory;
- local law restrictions;
- staff availability;
- terms acceptance;
- operational suitability.

Manual review must not be presented as financial approval or value confirmation.

## Staff Override

Staff may override eligibility decisions for safety, abuse prevention, compliance, operational errors, accessibility needs, or local-law constraints.

Every override should be recorded internally with:

- campaign ID;
- reason category;
- reviewer;
- timestamp;
- final decision;
- privacy-safe note for the public pilot report if material.

## Dispute Process

Participants should be able to submit a dispute within the campaign dispute window. The dispute process should define:

- where to submit the dispute;
- required information;
- review timeline;
- final decision authority;
- privacy handling;
- whether a correction report will be published.

Disputes do not create cash rights, compensation rights, property rights, or guaranteed access.

## Pilot Termination Process

The pilot may be paused or ended because of legal review, security concerns, operational constraints, abuse, low data quality, privacy concerns, or project governance decisions.

Termination should include:

- public notice;
- reason category;
- effective date;
- effect on open requests;
- final pilot report or cancellation note;
- confirmation that no sale, financial value, or mainnet claim is created by the termination.
