# Why A Hospitality Operator Might Test This Workflow

Status: conceptual operator explanation. No real hotel, operator, property, partner, active hospitality service, or real benefit is represented.

## Operational Problem

Small loyalty or community campaigns often combine rules, limited inventory, staff decisions, corrections, and outcome reporting. When those pieces live in separate messages or spreadsheets, it can be difficult to show which rule set applied and whether capacity was respected.

## What The Sandbox Demonstrates

- **Transparent campaign rules:** one dated rule set is visible before a simulation begins.
- **Controlled inventory:** approvals cannot exceed the mock capacity and inventory cannot become negative.
- **Loyalty eligibility:** a Sepolia-only test signal can support a mock staff review without becoming a booking workflow.
- **Public reporting:** aggregate outcomes can be hashed without publishing participant records.
- **Repeatable operations:** staff can repeat the create, review, decide, close, and report sequence.
- **Guest-data separation:** names, contact details, stay details, booking records, and payment information stay outside the public report and outside on-chain records.

## What It Does Not Demonstrate

The sandbox does not prove legal readiness, operator authority, property inventory, customer support, privacy compliance, production security, real guest demand, or a working hospitality integration. It is a local demonstration using fake data with no transaction broadcasting.

Before any real operator pilot, every gate in [`HOSPITALITY_OPERATIONS_GATE.md`](../HOSPITALITY_OPERATIONS_GATE.md) must have accountable evidence and approval.
