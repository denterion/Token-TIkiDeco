# TikiDeco One-Year Development Plan

Planning window: July 2026 through June 2027

Status: execution plan, not a launch promise. Timing depends on legal, privacy, security, governance, and hospitality-operator evidence. Mainnet, a token sale, active guest benefits, and V2 promotion remain unapproved.

## Year Objective

Prove that TikiDeco can operate as understandable, reproducible, and privacy-conscious hospitality loyalty infrastructure before making any production-network decision.

The year is successful if a cautious reviewer can verify the project, an independent reviewer can reproduce the V2 package, and a potential operator can test a limited off-chain workflow with fake or Sepolia-only data. Market activity is not a success metric.

## Quarter 1: Trust And Independent Review Intake

Window: July-September 2026

Primary outcome: establish the external review and legal decision paths.

Deliverables:

- keep the English public site, Trust Center, Project Facts, and release evidence synchronized;
- send the frozen V2 candidate package to selected independent reviewers for proposals;
- select counsel for entity, token classification, privacy, and hospitality terms review;
- run a documented Safe signer-loss and compromise drill using test-only data;
- turn every pilot live gate into an owned evidence task;
- publish monthly aggregate transparency updates.

Exit gate:

- reviewer scope, conflicts, deliverables, retest terms, and publication expectations are agreed in writing;
- counsel intake has named questions and a written engagement path;
- no unresolved V2 issue lacks an owner state, test link, or deployment impact;
- release evidence is reproducible from its exact source commit.

## Quarter 2: Remediation And Operator Sandbox

Window: October-December 2026

Primary outcome: validate the candidate architecture and the operational workflow without activating a benefit.

Deliverables:

- triage independent technical-review findings when a reviewer is engaged;
- implement narrow remediation PRs with regression tests and reviewer retest packages;
- exercise the local operator sandbox with fake campaigns, inventory, approvals, rejection, closure, and aggregate reports;
- define operator verification, support ownership, dispute handling, cancellation, and stop conditions;
- complete privacy data-flow review before any participant-data collection;
- decide the future Safe threshold and signer resilience model without changing it automatically.

Exit gate:

- no unresolved Critical or High technical finding is accepted for deployment;
- operator, inventory, terms, privacy, support, and incident responsibilities are explicit;
- sandbox reports contain aggregate fake/test data only;
- V2 remains non-canonical unless a separate reviewed promotion process begins.

## Quarter 3: Limited Sepolia Preview Decision

Window: January-March 2027

Primary outcome: decide whether a small, non-commercial Sepolia preview is supportable.

Conditional deliverables:

- publish campaign rules, threshold, snapshot or live-check mode, request window, and zero-or-limited inventory only after every configured reviewer approval exists;
- run read-only wallet balance checks with no transaction signing;
- collect only aggregate page, RPC, eligibility-result, comprehension, and feedback metrics;
- publish a privacy-safe pilot proof report and correction process;
- pause or close the preview when stop conditions are met.

Exit gate:

- legal, privacy, security, operations, and governance approvals are recorded with reviewer and timestamp;
- no raw wallet address is stored in analytics;
- no private guest data is collected;
- comprehension results show that participants understand Sepolia-only, no-sale, no-mainnet, no-live-benefit, V2-candidate, and audit-status boundaries;
- if any gate remains blocked, the campaign remains `draft-not-live` and the quarter ends with a documented no-go decision.

## Quarter 4: Production Decision, Not Automatic Launch

Window: April-June 2027

Primary outcome: produce a written go/no-go decision for the next architecture and operating model.

Deliverables:

- evaluate technical findings, pilot evidence, operator readiness, legal advice, privacy controls, treasury governance, support capacity, and incident drills;
- decide whether V2 should be remediated further, replaced, archived, or proposed for a new testnet deployment;
- define a production utility specification and token-distribution policy only if counsel and operations approve;
- refresh the threat model, audit scope, deployment runbook, and claims registry for the selected path;
- run `mainnet:check` as a decision gate, not a deployment command.

Exit gate:

- a signed internal decision records go, conditional go, or no-go;
- any mainnet discussion has external legal review, independent smart-contract audit evidence, treasury governance approval, incident readiness, final user terms, privacy process, and an identified operator;
- no deployment occurs as a consequence of this roadmap alone.

| Every month | Evidence |

| Dependency, claims, value, site, and security gates | CI artifacts and check summaries |
| Project Facts and Trust Center freshness review | Source-linked fact updates |
| Pilot live-gate review | Owner matrix and blocker register |
| Known issues and V2 scope review | Decision register and tests |
| Community feedback triage | Aggregate themes without private participant data |
| Treasury and Safe operational check | Test-only drill or documented review |

## Measurement Framework

Engineering and trust targets:

- zero new untriaged V2 Slither findings in blocking CI;
- all release-critical checks reproducible from an exact commit;
- every critical public fact linked to a source of truth;
- zero secrets or private participant datasets committed;
- zero transaction, token-purchase, or automatic-distribution paths on the public site;
- every external finding linked to triage, remediation, regression test, and retest status.

Product-learning targets for a future approved preview:

- measure completion and RPC failure rates without persistent identifiers;
- measure boundary comprehension only in aggregate;
- classify feedback into usability, translation, utility, privacy, and security themes;
- treat a blocked or cancelled preview as a valid result when evidence is insufficient.

## Required External Capabilities

The project cannot complete this plan through code alone. It requires:

- qualified external legal counsel;
- an independent Solidity reviewer or audit firm selected through the public criteria;
- independent Safe signers using separated devices and appropriate hardware protection;
- a verified hospitality operator before any real guest workflow;
- a privacy reviewer before collecting participant or guest data;
- named support and incident owners before any limited preview.

## Standing Stop Conditions

Stop promotion or preview work when:

- public wording implies sale, value, return, property, revenue, or unconditional hospitality rights;
- a private key, seed phrase, participant dataset, or guest record is exposed;
- a Critical or High finding affects the proposed workflow;
- Safe signer integrity or treasury control is uncertain;
- legal, privacy, security, operations, or governance approval is withdrawn;
- the project cannot support disputes, corrections, or incident communication.
